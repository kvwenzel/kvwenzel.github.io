Speaking

I began public speaking when I was 15, presenting for
350+ people 3x a week.

Now as a scientist, I've learned that my most important asset does not lie in my research, but in my ability and privilege to communicate and inspire. Scientific findings mean very little if no one can understand them.

I speak about a variety of topics centered around tech, design, psychology, and academia. Ocassionally I speak about cultural and social issues, bringing my unique multicultural background to light.

Talk #1

Technical errors can be annoying and frustrating, not to mention they diminish the usability of products... but the story doesn’t end there. Errors can also trigger threat responses similar to those experienced during encounters with racism. In this talk, I introduce my late-breaking research on racism and microaggressions in technology. This talk has been reformatted for a variety of audiences.

  • Research Labs
  • Product Teams
  • General Public
  • Students

Talk #2

Should you go to grad school? Should you choose a Master’s or PhD? What is a future in research like? What’s it like being a woman in STEM? In this talk, I walk students through everything they should consider before pursuing graduate school, adapted for a variety of ages.

  • Masters Students
  • Undergraduate Students
  • Secondary School Students
  • Research Associates
