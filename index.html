Present

I’m a second year HCI PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Geoff Kaufman. My current research focuses on (1) understanding allyship and responses to surveillance Post-Roe and (2) measuring and mitigating social disparities in language technologies.

Past

I previously studied usable privacy & security under Lorrie Cranor, Laura Dabbish, and Jason Hong.

Before beginning my PhD, I worked on some cool projects supporting education equity in STEM.

I completed my B.A. in computer science at Columbia University, Barnard College.
