Present

I’m a third year HCI PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Geoff Kaufman (eHeart Lab) and Laura Dabbish (CoEx Lab). My work investigates how various stakeholders (users, designers, developers) conceptualize and react to automated bias. I also build positive interventions to address these reactions. My main project focuses on language technology:

  1. Defining & Measuring Harm: What are users’ psychological responses to bias in language technology? How do these responses vary based on individual differences, especially across marginalized identities?
  2. Mitigating & Reversing Harm: How might we design to mitigate or reverse the psychological harm inflicted by bias in language technology?

Other research I’m excited about:

  • How might we evaluate the normative values embedded in various technologies? How might we persuade tech practitioners to design and build more responsibly?
  • What are the harms of generative AI, especially for marginalized groups? How might we design to mitigate these harms?
  • How have users’ perceptions of surveillance changed Post-Roe? How have users’ privacy and security behavior changed Post-Roe? How do these perceptions and behaviors vary across different gender, reproductive, and national identities?
